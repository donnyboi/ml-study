Evidence-based learning method

The Study Method

A cognitive science-backed framework for passing the AWS Machine Learning Associate exam — in less time, with deeper retention.
Built for Don Bopearachchi · AWS Certified Machine Learning — Associate (MLA-C01)
01 · CORE IDEA

Match the technique to the knowledge type

Most people study the wrong way — they re-read notes and highlight textbooks. It feels productive but doesn't stick. This method is different: it matches the type of knowledge you're learning to the technique that encodes it deepest.

The Principle

The brain doesn't remember what you read. It remembers what you do with what you read.

02 · THE FIVE KINDS OF KNOWLEDGE

Know what you're learning, know how to learn it

Not all exam content is the same. A service limit is different from a workflow. An algorithm name is different from "when to use it." Each requires a different tool.

Type 01

📖 Propositional — "Know-that"

Facts, definitions, service limits, numbers.
Best tools: Flashcards · Spaced Repetition · Cheat Sheets · Active Recall
Type 02

🛠️ Procedural — "Know-how"

Multi-step workflows, how to chain services together.
Best tools: Hands-on Labs · Process Diagrams · Teach It Out Loud · Runbooks
Type 03

🧭 Conditional — "Know-when-and-why"

Decision-making. Given scenario X, pick service Y. This is what the exam actually tests.
Best tools: Decision Trees · Practice Questions · Comparison Tables · Trigger-Word Maps
Type 04

✨ Tacit — "Feel-for-it"

Intuition from experience. Recognizing red flags, spotting wrong answers.
Best tools: Timed Practice Exams · Post-mortem Analysis · Case Studies · Peer Discussion
Type 05

🎯 Metacognitive — "Know your gaps"

Awareness of what you know and what you don't. The steering wheel of studying.
Best tools: Self-Assessments · Study Journal · Confidence Tagging · Spaced Review
03 · ENCODING — THE ENGINE

How information enters long-term memory

Encoding is the process of transforming information into memory. The deeper and more varied the encoding, the better the recall. Five encoding channels:

🧠
Semantic

Meaning-based. Connect new ideas to what you already know.

"Deep Archive = 180-day minimum because AWS discourages misuse."
🔗
Elaborative

Ask "why" and "how" for every fact.

"Why does this exist? What problem does it solve?"
🖼️
Visual

Diagrams, spatial layouts, mental models.

The filing-cabinet model for S3 storage classes.
🔊
Acoustic

Rhymes, mnemonics, absurd sentences.

"Glacier Deep, six months to keep."
✋
Kinesthetic

Physical action — clicking, typing, building.

Actually deploying a model in SageMaker Studio.
⚡
The Multiplier

Using 2+ channels for the same concept = 10x retention.

This is called "dual coding."
04 · THE ARSENAL

Eight techniques that actually work

Evidence-based, ranked by ROI for certification exams:

🎙️

1. Feynman Technique

Semantic + Elaborative encoding. Best for: complex concepts you think you understand.

Pick a concept → explain it to a 12-year-old → find gaps → refine → add analogy. The moment you can't explain it simply is the moment you learn you don't actually understand it.

🔍

2. Elaborative Interrogation

For every fact, ask WHY and HOW.
Example — S3 Deep Archive has a 180-day minimum

Why does it exist at all? → AWS has to amortize the cost of moving data to cold media.
Why 180 specifically? → The slowest media; longer commitment is needed to break even.
What does this tell me? → Only use it for data I truly won't need for 6+ months.

📦

3. Chunking

Group related items into meaningful clusters.
🔥 Hot🌡️ Warm❄️ Cold
StandardStandard-IAGlacier Instant
Intelligent-TieringOne Zone-IAGlacier Flexible
Deep Archive

Three chunks is easier to hold than seven items. Each chunk has its own rules.

🔀

4. Interleaving

Mix topics. Forces your brain to discriminate between concepts — which is exactly what the exam tests.
❌ Blocked (Bad)

2 hours on Domain 1, then 2 hours on Domain 2, then 2 hours on Domain 3.

✅ Interleaved (Good)

15 min D1, 15 min D3, 15 min D2, 15 min D4. Repeat.

🎭

5. Mnemonics

Encode arbitrary facts using sound, imagery, or absurdity.

Handling Missing Data: "Don't Ignore Missing Features Intentionally" → Drop · Impute · Model · Fill · Indicator

Pipeline Steps: "People Train Carefully, Release Models" → Processing · Training · Condition · Register · Model

🗺️

6. Concept Maps

Nodes + labeled arrows. Train relationships, not lists.

The exam tests relationships, not isolated facts. "If X, then Y" is a relationship question. Concept maps with labeled arrows like "detected by," "caused by," "responds to" train exactly that thinking.

🎨

7. Dual Coding

Written explanation + visual diagram. Two channels = deeper memory.

The rule: Every architecture concept gets a diagram. Every diagram gets a written paragraph. Never one without the other.

🎯

8. Retrieval Practice

Close everything. Write what you know. The struggle is the learning.

Struggling to recall strengthens the memory more than easy recall. Getting it wrong and correcting it is better than getting it right. Pair with spaced repetition.

08 · THE PRINCIPLES

Ten rules to remember

01
Re-reading is not studying

It feels productive. It isn't. Retrieval and production are what encode.

02
Match the technique to the knowledge type

Facts need flashcards. Workflows need labs. Decisions need practice questions.

03
Ask "why" for every fact

Elaborative interrogation turns arbitrary numbers into reasoned knowledge.

04
Use two encoding channels per concept

Written + visual. Or verbal + kinesthetic. Never just one.

05
Interleave, don't block

Mixing domains in one session feels harder — and it is. That's the point.

06
Struggle is the signal

When retrieval is hard, encoding is happening. Don't avoid the discomfort.

07
Teach it, or you don't know it

If you can't explain it to a 12-year-old, you haven't learned it yet.

08
Space your reviews

1 day → 3 days → 1 week → 2 weeks. Forgetting is part of learning.

09
Track your gaps

You can only fix what you can see. Log weak topics. Revisit them.

10
Trust the process

This method feels slower. The retention is dramatically higher. Stay the course.
